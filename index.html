Lecturer


Shanghai Institute of Technology



Profile

I am a spatial ecologist with particular interests in the distributions, diversity and abundance of species and currently find myself most happy to work at the intersection of urban and road ecology. My recent research interests mainly focus on exploring interdisciplinary approaches with the aim to improve our understanding of human impacts on natural world.


I am also a health geographer in the Population, Health and Wellbeing (PHeW) research group at the University of Southampton interested in understanding the relationships between human health and social, built and natural environments. I have long standing interests in using geospatial approaches to tackle public health issues linked to environmental factors, particularly those involving water, sanitation and hygiene in developing countries.


I am now a lecturer at the School of Ecological Technology and Engineering (SETE), Shanghai Institute of Technology.

Research Interests

  • Water, sanitation and hygiene
  • Spatial demography
  • GIS and remote sensing
  • Agent based modelling
